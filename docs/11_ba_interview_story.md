# 11. BA Interview Story

## 1. Short Project Pitch

InternshipHub is a Business Analyst portfolio case study for an internship recruitment management system.

The system helps companies manage the internship recruitment process from candidate application submission to interview scheduling, feedback collection, and final hiring decision.

I built this project to practice the full BA workflow, including stakeholder analysis, process modeling, requirement documentation, user stories, acceptance criteria, wireframes, test scenarios, and traceability.

## 2. Why I Chose This Project

I chose this project because internship recruitment is a realistic business process and easy to understand. It involves many stakeholders such as candidates, HR recruiters, interviewers, hiring managers, admins, developers, and QA engineers.

It also has clear business problems: manual tracking, unclear candidate status, unstructured feedback, and lack of pipeline visibility.

## 3. What I Did as a Business Analyst

In this project, I worked through the BA process step by step:

1. I defined the business problem and project scope.
2. I identified stakeholders and analyzed their goals and pain points.
3. I documented the AS-IS process to understand the current workflow.
4. I designed the TO-BE process to show the improved workflow.
5. I wrote the BRD to capture business requirements.
6. I wrote the SRS to define detailed software requirements.
7. I converted requirements into user stories and product backlog items.
8. I wrote acceptance criteria using Given-When-Then format.
9. I created wireframe specifications for the main screens.
10. I prepared test scenarios for QA validation.
11. I created a Requirement Traceability Matrix to connect requirements to test scenarios.

## 4. Main Business Problem

The current internship recruitment process is often fragmented across email, spreadsheets, chat tools, and manual notes.

This creates several problems:

- HR spends too much time tracking candidate status manually.
- Candidates do not know their current application status.
- Interviewers may receive candidate information late or in different formats.
- Feedback is not standardized.
- Hiring managers do not have a clear pipeline view.

## 5. Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is a centralized system called InternshipHub.

The system allows candidates to submit applications and track their status. HR can manage candidates, update status, and schedule interviews. Interviewers can submit structured feedback. Hiring managers can review the pipeline and approve final decisions.

## 6. What I Learned

This project helped me understand that a BA does not only write documents. A BA needs to understand the business problem, ask the right questions, clarify unclear requirements, document the solution, and make sure developers and QA engineers can build and test the correct product.

I also learned that traceability is important because it connects business goals with functional requirements, user stories, acceptance criteria, and test scenarios.

## 7. Interview Answer: What is a Business Analyst?

A Business Analyst is the bridge between business stakeholders and the technical team. The BA understands business problems, gathers and analyzes requirements, documents the expected solution, and supports the team during development and testing.

In a software project, the BA helps make sure the final product solves the real business need, not only technical tasks.

## 8. Interview Answer: How Do You Gather Requirements?

I gather requirements by identifying stakeholders, preparing questions, interviewing users, analyzing current documents, observing the current process, and validating the future process with stakeholders.

In my InternshipHub project, I identified candidates, HR recruiters, interviewers, hiring managers, admins, developers, and QA engineers. Then I analyzed their goals, pain points, and responsibilities.

## 9. Interview Answer: What is the Difference Between BRD and SRS?

A BRD focuses on business needs and objectives. It explains what the business wants to achieve and why the project is needed.

An SRS converts those business needs into detailed software requirements. It explains what the system must do, including functional requirements, non-functional requirements, user roles, validation rules, permission rules, and system behavior.

## 10. Interview Answer: What is AS-IS and TO-BE?

AS-IS describes how the current business process works before the new solution.

TO-BE describes how the future process should work after the new solution is implemented.

In InternshipHub, the AS-IS process uses email, spreadsheets, and chat tools. The TO-BE process centralizes candidate applications, status tracking, interview scheduling, feedback, and hiring decisions in one system.

## 11. Interview Answer: How Do You Write User Stories?

I write user stories from the user's perspective using this format:

```text
As a [user role], I want [goal], so that [business value].
```

For example:

```text
As a candidate, I want to view my application status, so that I can know my recruitment progress.
```

This format helps the team understand who needs the feature, what the user wants, and why it matters.

## 12. Interview Answer: What are Acceptance Criteria?

Acceptance criteria are the conditions that a user story must satisfy to be accepted.

I usually write acceptance criteria using Given-When-Then format because it is clear and testable.

Example:

```gherkin
Given I am logged in as a candidate
When I upload a PDF or DOCX file under 5MB
Then the system saves the CV and shows an upload success message
```

## 13. Interview Answer: What is a Requirement Traceability Matrix?

A Requirement Traceability Matrix connects business requirements, functional requirements, user stories, acceptance criteria, and test scenarios.

It helps the team check whether every requirement is covered and testable. It also helps analyze the impact when requirements change.

## 14. Strong Points of This Project

The strong points of this project are:

- It includes both business-level and system-level requirements.
- It connects requirements to user stories and test scenarios.
- It includes AS-IS and TO-BE process analysis.
- It shows how BA supports development and QA.
